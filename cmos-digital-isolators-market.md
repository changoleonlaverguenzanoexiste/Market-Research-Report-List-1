<p><p>CMOS digital isolators are devices that provide galvanic isolation and data transmission between different circuits while using complementary metal-oxide-semiconductor (CMOS) technology. These isolators offer benefits such as lower power consumption, shorter propagation delay, improved noise immunity, and they are ROHS compliant.</p><p>The CMOS digital isolators market is witnessing significant growth due to several factors. One of the key drivers is the increasing adoption of these isolators in various industrial applications, such as motor controls, power supplies, and factory automation, where isolation is essential to ensure safety and reliability. Additionally, the rising demand for high-speed data transmission with enhanced noise immunity is fueling market growth.</p><p>Another factor driving the market is the growing trend of replacing traditional optocouplers with CMOS digital isolators. Optocouplers have limitations such as slower data transmission speeds, susceptibility to aging and degradation, and high power consumption. CMOS digital isolators overcome these challenges, making them a preferred choice for many applications.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing a surge in demand from the automotive sector, driven by the increasing integration of advanced electronics in vehicles. <p><p>CMOS Digital Isolators are electronic devices used to separate two circuits while allowing digital information transfer between them. Unidirectional Digital Isolators facilitate data transmission in one direction, either from the input to the output or vice versa. On the other hand, Bidirectional Digital Isolators enable data exchange in both directions simultaneously. These isolators find application in various industries, such as medical, industrial, automotive, and telecommunications, ensuring secure communication and preventing noise, voltage spikes, and electromagnetic interference between connected circuits.</p></p>
