<p><ul><li>Positive Pole</li><li>Negative Pole</li><li>Diaphragm</li><li>Electrolyte</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Flexible lithium-ion batteries consist of several components that make up the market types. The positive pole, also known as the cathode, is responsible for the battery's storage. The negative pole, also known as the anode, acts as the source of charge. The diaphragm, or separator, prevents direct contact between the cathode and anode while allowing the flow of lithium ions. The electrolyte, a conductive solution, facilitates the movement of ions between the cathode and anode. All these components play crucial roles in the functioning and development of flexible lithium-ion batteries.</p></p>
