<p><p>Calcium chloride food grade is available in two main types: dihydrate and anhydrous. Dihydrate calcium chloride contains two molecules of water, while anhydrous calcium chloride is completely free of water. Dihydrate is commonly used in food preservation and as a firming agent, while anhydrous is often preferred for specialized applications where the absence of water is important. Both forms are widely used in the food industry for various purposes due to their ability to enhance texture, flavor, and shelf life of food products.</p></p>
