<p><p>Recombinant vaccines are a type of vaccine that uses genetic engineering techniques to produce specific proteins or antigens from the pathogen. There are three main types: subunit recombinant vaccines, attenuated recombinant vaccines, and vector recombinant vaccines. Subunit recombinant vaccines only include specific protein fragments of the pathogen, attenuated recombinant vaccines use a weakened form of the pathogen, and vector recombinant vaccines use a harmless virus or bacteria as a carrier to deliver the genetic material of the pathogen. Each type offers a different approach to immunization against various diseases.</p></p>
