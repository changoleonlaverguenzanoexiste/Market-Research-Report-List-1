<p><p>Neuromodulation refers to the use of medical devices to stimulate or regulate the nervous system to alleviate symptoms or treat various conditions. Internal treatment involves the implantation of small devices, such as spinal cord stimulators or deep brain stimulators, directly into the body to provide targeted stimulation. External treatment involves non-invasive techniques, such as transcranial magnetic stimulation or transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation, which deliver stimulation externally without the need for surgery. Both internal and external neuromodulation methods are utilized in the medical field to address a range of neurological disorders and chronic pain conditions.</p></p>
