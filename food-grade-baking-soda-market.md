<p><p>The food-grade baking soda market can be categorized into three types: Sodium Carbonate Method, Trona Method, and Ammonium Carbonate Method. In the Sodium Carbonate Method, baking soda is produced through a chemical reaction between sodium carbonate and carbon dioxide. The Trona Method involves extracting baking soda from trona ore, which is a naturally occurring mineral. Lastly, the Ammonium Carbonate Method utilizes a reaction between ammonium carbonate and carbon dioxide to produce baking soda. These different methods provide various options for producing food-grade baking soda in the market.</p></p>
