<p><p>Vaccines for Children (VFC) market offers various types of vaccines to protect children from infectious diseases. Recombinant vaccines are developed by inserting genes into a vector and are effective against diseases like Hepatitis B and HPV. Viral vaccines use weakened or inactivated viruses to build immunity, including MMR and Varicella vaccines. Polysaccharide vaccines are based on sugar molecules from bacteria, preventing diseases like meningococcal meningitis. Lastly, there are other types of vaccines, such as toxoid vaccines (like Tetanus) and conjugate vaccines (like Pneumococcal), each designed to protect against specific diseases.</p></p>
