<p><p>Smart cookers, also known as smart multicookers or smart slow cookers, are kitchen appliances that use advanced technology to automate the cooking process. These appliances are equipped with various sensors, timers, and programmed settings that allow users to easily cook a wide range of dishes with minimal effort.</p><p>The smart cookers market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years, primarily driven by the growing consumer demand for convenient cooking solutions. The increasing popularity of smart homes and connected devices has also played a crucial role in bolstering the market growth. Additionally, the rise in disposable income, changing consumer lifestyles, and the growing trend of healthy eating have contributed to the increased adoption of smart cookers.</p><p>One of the major trends in the smart cookers market is the integration of smart features with existing cooking appliances. Many manufacturers are now developing smart cookers that can be connected to smartphones or other devices, allowing users to control and monitor the cooking process remotely. These smart cookers can be programmed to start cooking at a specific time, adjust temperature and cooking time, and even provide recipe suggestions based on the ingredients available.</p><p>Another emerging trend is the incorporation of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) technologies into smart cookers. These advancements enable the cookers to learn users' preferences and cooking habits over time, resulting in personalized cooking experiences and improved efficiency.</p><p>Overall, the smart cookers market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.90% during the forecast period. The increasing adoption of smart homes, advancements in technology, and the demand for convenient cooking solutions are expected to drive this growth.</p></p>
