<p><p>GPS tracking devices are divided into two main market types: satellite and cellular. Satellite tracking devices use signals from satellites to determine the location of an object or a person. These devices are ideal for remote and off-grid areas where cellular coverage may be limited. On the other hand, cellular tracking devices utilize cellular networks to transmit location data. They are commonly used in urban areas with strong network coverage. Both types of devices offer real-time tracking and a wide range of applications such as fleet management, personal tracking, and asset tracking.</p></p>
