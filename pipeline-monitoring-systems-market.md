These advancements enable remote monitoring and control of pipelines, predictive maintenance, and real-time data analytics, leading to improved safety, reduced operational costs, and enhanced overall performance.</p><p>Moreover, stringent government regulations and standards regarding pipeline safety and integrity are boosting the adoption of pipeline monitoring systems. <p><p>Pipeline monitoring systems can be categorized into two main types: metallic and non-metallic. Metallic pipeline monitoring systems are designed for pipelines made of metal materials. These systems use sensors and detectors to monitor the physical integrity and detect any leakages or damages in the metallic pipes. On the other hand, non-metallic pipeline monitoring systems are tailored for pipelines made of non-metal materials such as plastic or composite. These systems employ different technologies to ensure the proper functioning and safety of non-metallic pipelines. Both types of systems play a crucial role in maintaining the integrity and efficiency of pipeline infrastructure.</p></p>
