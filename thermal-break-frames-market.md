<p><p>Thermal break frames refer to frames that are designed to minimize heat transfer or thermal conductivity between the external and internal parts of a building. These frames are typically used in windows, doors, and facades, and are made of materials such as aluminum, fiberglass, or polyurethane. The thermal break technology involves the insertion of a non-metallic material between the two metal sections of the frame to create a barrier that reduces heat transfer. This helps in improving the energy efficiency of buildings and reducing heat loss or gain.</p><p>The future outlook of the thermal break frames market is promising, with a projected growth rate of 7.1% during the forecasted period.
