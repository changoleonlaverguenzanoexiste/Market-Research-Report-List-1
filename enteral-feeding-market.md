<p><p>Enteral feeding, also known as tube feeding, is a method of providing nutrition directly into the gastrointestinal tract through a tube. It is typically used for patients who are unable to consume food orally or have impaired swallowing abilities. The tube is either inserted through the nose or mouth and passed down to the stomach or small intestine, or it is placed directly into the stomach through a surgical procedure.</p><p>The enteral feeding market has been witnessing steady growth in recent years, driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, rising geriatric population, and advancements in medical technology. Additionally, the growing awareness about the benefits of enteral nutrition, such as reduced risk of aspiration pneumonia and improved patient outcomes, has further fueled market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has also had a positive impact on the enteral feeding market. The virus has significantly increased the demand for enteral feeding devices and supplies, especially in hospitals and long-term care facilities as they play a crucial role in the nutrition management of critically ill patients.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the enteral feeding market include the development of advanced enteral feeding devices that offer improved patient comfort and ease of use.
