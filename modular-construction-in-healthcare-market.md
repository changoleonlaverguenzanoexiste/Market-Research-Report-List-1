<p><p>Modular construction in the healthcare market refers to the method of constructing healthcare facilities using prefabricated modules that are manufactured off-site and then assembled on-site. Permanent modular construction involves building structures that are intended to remain in place permanently, providing long-term healthcare solutions. Relocatable modular construction, on the other hand, refers to structures designed to be easily moved or relocated, allowing for flexibility in response to changing healthcare needs. Both types offer advantages such as cost-effectiveness, shorter construction times, and customizable design options to meet specific healthcare requirements.</p></p>
