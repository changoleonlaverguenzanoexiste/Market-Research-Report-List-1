<p><p>The orthopedic implant market comprises different types of implants, including metallic, ceramic, polymeric, and others. Metallic implants, typically made of titanium or stainless steel, offer strength and durability. Ceramic implants are known for their biocompatibility and ability to provide a natural joint feel. Polymeric implants, often made from materials like polyethylene or polycarbonate, offer flexibility and resistance to wear. The "others" category includes implants made from materials like carbon fiber-reinforced polymers or bioabsorbable materials, providing unique advantages such as lightness or the ability to degrade over time.</p></p>
