<p><p>The glassine market is segmented into two types: bleached glassine paper and unbleached glassine paper. Bleached glassine paper undergoes a bleaching process using chemicals, resulting in a bright white appearance. This type of glassine is commonly used in applications that require a clean, smooth, and attractive finish, such as packaging, labels, and envelopes. On the other hand, unbleached glassine paper remains in its natural state without any bleaching treatment. It is often preferred for applications where a more natural and eco-friendly look is desired, such as bakery release liners and food packaging.</p></p>
