<p><p>Secondary switchgears are essential electrical distribution devices used to protect power systems and control the flow of electricity. Gas-insulated switchgears utilize sulfur hexafluoride gas to provide insulation, ensuring high reliability and safety. Vacuum-insulated switchgears, on the other hand, use vacuum technology to offer insulation, suitable for medium voltage applications. Both types are designed to minimize the risks associated with electrical faults and enable efficient power management. These switchgears play a crucial role in various industries, including power generation, utilities, commercial buildings, and industrial facilities.</p></p>
