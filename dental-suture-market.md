<p><p>The dental suture market is divided into two types: absorbable and non-absorbable sutures. Absorbable sutures are made from materials that break down over time and are gradually absorbed by the body. These sutures do not require removal and are commonly used in oral surgeries. On the other hand, non-absorbable sutures are made from materials that do not break down and must be removed after a period of time. These sutures are often used in dental procedures where prolonged support or closure is necessary.</p></p>
