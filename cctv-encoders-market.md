<p><p>CCTV (Closed Circuit Television) encoders are devices used to convert analog video signals into digital format for transmission over IP-based networks. They come in various types, including 1 channel, 2 channels, 4 channels, 8 channels, 16 channels, and others. These types indicate the number of video channels that can be connected to the encoder for processing and transmission. For example, a 1-channel encoder can handle one video signal, while a 16-channel encoder can process up to sixteen video signals simultaneously. The different channel options cater to the varying needs of video surveillance systems of different sizes and complexities.</p></p>
