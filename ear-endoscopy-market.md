<p><p>Ear endoscopy is a medical procedure that allows doctors to examine the middle and inner ear using an endoscope, a thin tube with a light and camera. Middle ear endoscopy focuses on the examination of the middle ear, which includes the eardrum and the small bones that transmit sound. Inner ear endoscopy focuses on the examination of the inner ear, including the cochlea and the vestibular system responsible for balance. These types of endoscopies help diagnose and treat various ear disorders such as infections, hearing loss, and vertigo.</p></p>
