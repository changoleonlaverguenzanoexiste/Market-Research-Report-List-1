<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Inspection drones are unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) used for various inspection purposes. They can be categorized into two main types: fixed-wing and rotor-wing drones. </p><p>Fixed-wing drones have a design similar to airplanes, with a fixed wing and propulsion system that enables them to fly straight and cover large areas efficiently. These drones are suitable for long-distance inspections, such as power line monitoring or environmental surveys, as they can stay airborne for an extended period.</p><p>On the other hand, rotor-wing drones, also known as multirotor drones, have multiple rotors that provide vertical takeoff and landing capabilities. These drones can hover in a stationary position and maneuver in tight spaces, making them ideal for close-range inspections and intricate tasks like building inspections or infrastructure maintenance.</p><p>Both fixed-wing and rotor-wing drones have their advantages and applications, allowing industries to choose the appropriate type according to their specific inspection requirements.</p></p>
