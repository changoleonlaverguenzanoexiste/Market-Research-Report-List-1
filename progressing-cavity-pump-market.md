<p><p>A Progressing Cavity Pump (PCP) is a type of positive displacement pump that uses a helical rotor and stator to move fluids efficiently. It is also known as a single-screw pump or eccentric screw pump. The PCP is designed to handle a wide range of fluids, including highly viscous, abrasive, and shear-sensitive substances. The pump operates by creating a cavity between the rotor and stator, allowing the fluid to progress from the suction end to the discharge end.</p><p>The Progressing Cavity Pump market has been witnessing significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue this trend during the forecast period. A major factor driving the market growth is the increasing demand for PCPs in various industries, such as oil and gas, wastewater treatment, food and beverage, and chemical processing. PCPs are preferred over other pump types due to their ability to handle challenging fluids and offer higher efficiency and reliability.</p><p>Additionally, the rising focus on improving operational efficiency and reducing maintenance costs is propelling the adoption of PCPs.
