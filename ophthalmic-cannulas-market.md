<p><p>Ophthalmic cannulas are used in eye surgeries to deliver fluids, medications, or to remove fluids. They are available in two main types: curved and straight cannulas. Curved cannulas are designed with a curved tip, allowing for easy access to tighter areas or angles during delicate procedures. Straight cannulas, on the other hand, have a straight tip and are often used for standard eye surgeries. Both types provide precise control and are essential tools in ophthalmic surgeries.</p></p>

<p><p>Ophthalmic cannulas are commonly used in various applications within the field of ophthalmology. These applications include irrigation, injection, suction, and others. Irrigation involves using a cannula to deliver a controlled flow of fluid to wash the eye during surgery or other procedures. Injection refers to the use of a cannula to administer medications or fluids directly into the eye. Suction employs a cannula to remove excess fluids or blood from the eye. Other applications encompass tasks like transferring fluids, accessing the anterior chamber, or facilitating vitrectomy procedures.</p></p>
