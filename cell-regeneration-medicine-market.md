<p><p>Cell regeneration medicine, also known as regenerative medicine, refers to the field of medicine focused on repairing, replacing, or regenerating diseased or damaged cells, tissues, or organs to restore normal function. Advancements in stem cell research and tissue engineering technologies have played a vital role in the development of regenerative medicine. These technologies have enabled researchers to create functional tissues, organs, and even body parts in the laboratory, which can be transplanted or used for drug testing and disease modeling purposes.</p><p>Moreover, the rising prevalence of chronic diseases, such as cardiovascular disorders, orthopedic ailments, diabetes, and cancer, has fueled the demand for regenerative medicine treatments. Cell-based therapies offer promising solutions for treating these diseases by stimulating natural healing processes and restoring proper tissue function.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing geriatric population, coupled with the growing demand for organ transplantation, has also contributed to the market growth.
