<p><p>Electronystagmography (ENG) is a diagnostic test that assesses the balance and coordination of eye movements. It includes various types of testing methods such as Videonystagmography (VNG), which uses video goggles to monitor eye movements; Rotary Chair Test, which measures eye movements in response to stimulated rotations; Computerized Dynamic Visual Acuity (DVA), which evaluates vision clarity during head movements; Computerized Dynamic Posturography (CDP), which measures balance responses; Subjective Visual Vertical, which assesses perception of verticality; and Vestibular Evoked Myogenic Potential (VEMP), which tests the inner ear's response to sound. These tests aid in diagnosing and managing vestibular disorders.</p></p>
