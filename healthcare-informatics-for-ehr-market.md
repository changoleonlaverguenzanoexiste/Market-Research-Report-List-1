<p><p>Healthcare informatics refers to the management and analysis of healthcare data using technology. In the context of electronic health record (EHR) market, there are two main types: cloud-based and on-premise. Cloud-based EHRs store patient data on remote servers, allowing for easy accessibility and reducing the need for physical infrastructure. On-premise EHRs, on the other hand, require local servers and IT resources for data storage and management. Both types have their pros and cons, with cloud-based offering scalability and cost-effectiveness, while on-premise provides greater control and customization.</p></p>
